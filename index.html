    Stellar Parallax

    Stellar parallax is a form of simple parallax. Parallax is a mixture of motion and light physics. It is the apparent displacement of an object due to the change of the viewers perspective. For example, think of a person standing in front a wall. As you moved your point of perspective, meaning you either bend or twist, the wall behind the person seems to move. This is apparent displacement, or displacement of something you can see. this is parallax. A better example would be what you see outside the car window Now, we take this concept of parallax and apply it to space. The basis of everthing scientists do with stellar parallax is based on distance in one way or the other. As of now there two ways of finding distance. More on distance will come on the next page.
